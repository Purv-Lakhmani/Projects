Bald Eagle

The majestic and powerful bird of prey, symbol of freedom

Overview

The Bald Eagle (Haliaeetus leucocephalus) is a large bird of prey native to North America. It is easily recognizable by its white head and tail, brown body, and yellow beak. The Bald Eagle is the national bird and symbol of the United States.

Habitat

Bald Eagles are typically found near large bodies of open water such as lakes, rivers, and coastal regions. They prefer habitats with abundant fish populations and large trees for nesting.

Diet

Bald Eagles primarily feed on fish, which they catch using their sharp talons. They also eat birds, small mammals, and carrion. Bald Eagles are known to scavenge and steal food from other birds.

Behavior and Social Interaction

Bald Eagles are solitary and territorial birds, especially during the breeding season. They build large nests, called eyries, in tall trees or on cliffs. These birds are known for their spectacular courtship displays, including aerial acrobatics.

Interaction with Humans

Bald Eagles are not harmful to humans and are often admired for their strength and beauty. They have been the focus of conservation efforts, which have helped their populations recover from the brink of extinction. The Bald Eagle is a symbol of freedom and resilience.
